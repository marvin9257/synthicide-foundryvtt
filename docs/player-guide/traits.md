# Traits

## Purpose

Manage milestone traits and generated feature traits without surprises.

## Before You Start

1. Open your actor sheet.
2. Confirm whether you are editing a milestone trait, bioclass trait, or aspect trait.

## Steps

1. Open the Traits tab for standard milestone traits.
2. Add a trait at the appropriate level section.
3. Open the trait item and enter details.
4. Use the Bioclass tab for bioclass-generated traits.
5. Use the Aspect tab for aspect-generated traits.
6. Edit generated traits when a choice or value entry is required.
7. Save trait edits, then confirm actor values.

## What Happens Automatically

1. Standard traits are grouped into milestone levels.
2. Bioclass and aspect traits are created automatically when those items are assigned.
3. Trait lists update when features are replaced.

Example: if you replace a bioclass, bioclass-generated trait entries are rebuilt for the new bioclass.

## What You Must Set Manually

1. Trait text content and table-specific details.
2. Choice-based trait options.
3. Any manual follow-up the trait description requires.

Example: if a trait says "pick one defense to increase," that choice must be made in the trait and saved.

## Limitations and Not Implemented

1. Standard trait levels are limited to milestone levels 1, 4, and 7 (you cannot add a normal trait at level 2, 3, 5, or 6).
2. Generated trait effects that depend on choices may not finalize until you edit and save the trait (example: "choose one" branches).
3. Trait outcomes that depend on scene context are manual (example: a situational bonus trait still needs player/GM judgment for when it applies).

## Troubleshooting

### Q: A trait effect is missing. How do I get it to apply?

A: Open the trait, make required choices, and save.

### Q: My trait list looks stale after feature changes. How do I refresh it?

A: Reopen the actor sheet after the feature change is complete.

### Q: Why can I not add a trait at the level I want?

A: Standard traits are limited to milestone levels 1, 4, and 7.
