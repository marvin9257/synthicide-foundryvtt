# Aspects

## Purpose

Assign and manage your aspect, including abilities and generated aspect traits.

## Before You Start

1. Open your actor sheet.
2. Make sure an aspect item is available in your Items directory or compendium.
3. Confirm your table rules for when aspect abilities are descriptive vs mechanical.

## Steps

1. Drag an aspect item onto your actor sheet.
2. Open the Aspect tab and confirm the aspect name appears.
3. Review listed aspect abilities.
4. Review generated aspect traits.
5. Open each generated trait and make required choices.
6. Save each edited trait so selected effects are finalized.
7. If changing aspects, drag a replacement aspect onto the actor.
8. Re-check aspect traits and any affected values after replacement.

## What Happens Automatically

1. The actor can only hold one aspect at a time.
2. Assigning a new aspect replaces the previous one.
3. Aspect-linked traits are generated and attached automatically with the aspect.

Example: replacing your aspect removes the previous aspect entry, then creates the new linked aspect traits.

## What You Must Set Manually

1. Trait choices inside generated aspect traits.
2. Any aspect text details your table expects to track explicitly.
3. Confirmation of any non-automated effect handling during play.

Example: if an aspect trait asks you to pick one option, open the trait, pick the option, and save.

## Limitations and Not Implemented

1. Multi-aspect support is not implemented, so one actor can only have one active aspect.
2. Choice-based trait effects are not always finalized on creation (example: if the trait says choose one attribute, you must open and save that trait choice).
3. Aspect text that depends on scene context is manual at resolution time (example: if an ability grants situational narrative advantage, add that modifier when you make the roll).
4. If an aspect references a rules-kit subsystem that has no dedicated sheet automation, resolve that outcome with player/GM adjudication each time it triggers.

## Troubleshooting

### Q: An aspect effect is missing. What should I do?

A: Open the generated aspect trait, make any required choice, and save it.

### Q: I replaced an aspect but old details are still showing. How do I refresh it?

A: Reopen the actor sheet and verify the active aspect entry is correct.

### Q: It still looks wrong after that. What is the fallback?

A: Remove and re-apply the intended aspect, then re-check traits and values.
